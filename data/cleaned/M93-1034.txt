2:57	The reports are ordered by language/domain (EJV, JJV, EME, JME) and secondarily by site (in alphabetical order).
3:57	Deeper investigation into system performance may be done on the basis of template-by-template scor e reports, which detail the performance of the system on each article in the test set . The template-by-template score reports are not included in this appendix . A brief introduction to reading the score reports is presented here . Some information on the performance metrics may also be found in the paper, "Tipster/MUC-5 Information Extraction System Evaluation" by B . Sundheim in this volume . Further description of the score report and further definition and explanation of the scoring categories and performance metrics may be found in this volume in the paper titled "MUC-5 Evaluatio n Metrics" by N. Chinchor and B. Sundheim . The first column in the report, SLOT, contains one of the following three types of label: A slot name, e.g., content An object name, e.g., <template> The word "subtotals" Of the three types of rows, the one of primary interest is the one labeled with a slot name . The rows labeled with an object name may be disregarded.
4:57	The rows containing subtotals may be of interest to those interested in performance on the various types of objects in the template . The following tallies for the test set are presented in columns in the score reports : POS Number possible (= COR+PAR+INC+MIS), i .e. , the number of fills a system should generate.
5:57	This value is somewhat variable among systems because of alternative cardinalities of slot fills allowed for in the answer key . ACT Number "actual" (= COR+PAR+INC+SPU), i .e. , the number of fills generated by the system under evaluation C OR Number correct PAR Number partially correct IN C Number incorrect S P U Number spuriou s MIS Number missing NON Number "noncommittal," i.e., number of null slot fills generated by system, where the fill is also null in the answer ke y The rightmost columns in the report contain the scores for the following extraction metrics: ERR Error per response fill = (.SPAR+INC+SPU+MIS)/(COR+PAR+INC+SPU+MIS ) UND Undergeneration = MIS/P O S OVG Overgeneration = SPU/AC T SUB Substitution = (.SPAR+INC)/(COR+PAR+INC ) REC Recall = (COR+.5PAR)/PO S PRE Precision = (COR+.SPAR)/ACT None of the above metrics includes the NON scoring category in its computation . Readers who wish to consider noncommittal fills to be correct may revise the formulas by including NON in all denominators and i n those numerators where COR appears . Note, however, that the high undergeneration scores obtained by mos t systems on many slots in the evaluation places in question the value of considering noncommittal fills to b e correct.
6:57	36 2 The first two rows at the bottom of the report are the ALL-OBJECTS and MATCHED-ONLY rows.
7:57	These offer two options in the way the ERR, UND, OVG, SUB, REC, and PRE scores are computed.
8:57	ALL-OBJECTS gives the official overall tallies and overall ERR, UND, OVG, SUB, REC, and PRE scores.
9:57	The tallies in POS, ACT, COR, PAR, INC, SPU, MIS, and NON columns add up to the ALL-OBJECTS values, but not all rows are considered.
10:57	The rows that are not considered arc th e object rows (i.e. , those where the entry in the SLOT column is an object name), the subtotal rows (sinc e they simply sum up the tallies for the scored slots in each object), and the following slot rows in th e <template> object: doc-nr, doc-date, and doc-source (these appear only in the EJV and JJV fulltemplate reports).
11:57	MATCHED-ONLY contains tallies and scores given a method of scoring that eliminates slot-fill penalties in the case where the system under evaluation failed to generate an expected object and in the cas e where it generated a spurious object.
12:57	The tallies in the slot rows do not sum up to the totals contained in the MATCHED-ONLY row.
13:57	The scores for the metrics in the ALL-OBJECTS and MATCHED-ONLY rows are not computed by averaging the scores in the individual slot rows, but instead are computed on the basis of the tally totals containe d in the ALL-OBJECTS or MATCHED-ONLY row.
14:57	The remaining rows at the bottom of the report contain scores for the following metrics : TEXT-FILTERING Text filtering (document detection ) RICHNESS-NORMALIZED-ERROR Richness-normalized error (normalized version o f error per response fill) ERROR-RATE-PER-WORD Error rate per word F-MEASURES F-measures (weighted combination of recall an d precision) The formulation of each of the above metrics is described below.
15:57	Text-filtering scores are computed on the basis of relevant/nonrelevant decisions made by th e system for the text as a whole, not on the basis of tallies in the slot rows . Some of the score category columns are used to contain the tallies for these decisions across the test set : COR (decides relevant and relevant is correct), SPU (decides relevant and nonrelevant is correct), MIS (decides nonrelevan t and relevant is correct), and NON (decides nonrelevant and nonrelevant is correct) . Text-filtering formulas are therefore as follows : REC = COR/(COR+MIS); PRE = COR/(COR+SPU) ; UND = MIS/(COR+MIS); OVG = SPU/(COR+SPU) . Two values of richness-normalized error are computed . The formulas are as follows : Min-err = Wrong/All-fills, Max-err = Wrong/Req-fills . There will be a range of fill counts from the minimum number of fills required (Req-fills) to the maximum number of fills allowed (All-fills), according to the answer key . These values are almost, but not quite, system-independent . Error rate per word is computed as Wrong/Word-count . The scores for Japanese are not accurate . F-measure scores corresponding to three different weightings of precision relative to recall arc computed.
16:57	The P&R value weights precision the same as recall ; the 2P&R value gives twice th e weight to precision ; the P&2R value weights precision half as much as recall . The general formul a is F = ((32+1 .0)xPxR/(6 2xP)+R, where is the weighting factor.
